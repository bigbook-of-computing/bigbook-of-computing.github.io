Chapter 1

Foundations of Stochastic Simulation


  • Address the "Curse of Dimensionality" in the partition function $Z = \sum_{\mathbf{s}} e^{-\beta E(\mathbf{s})}$.
  • Introduce Importance Sampling to focus computation on high-probability (low-energy) states.
  • Establish Markov Chains, detailed balance, and ergodicity as the theoretical foundation.
  • Derive the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm as the core engine for sampling the Boltzmann distribution.

Physics II: Lattice Gauge Theory


  • Simulate Quantum Field Theories (QFT) non-perturbatively by discretizing 4D spacetime.
  • Define the Wilson Action $S_W$ as the "energy," replacing simple spins with $SU(N)$ matrices on links.
  • Implement a Metropolis simulation to update $SU(N)$ matrices and sample the path integral.
  • Measure the Wilson Loop $\langle W(R, T) \rangle$ to demonstrate the "area law" as proof of quark confinement.

Chapter 6

Advanced Monte Carlo Methods


  • Solve problems where Metropolis fails: critical slowing down near $T_c$ and trapping in local minima.
  • Implement Cluster Algorithms (e.g., Wolff) to flip entire correlated domains and beat slowing down.
  • Use Parallel Tempering (Replica Exchange) to swap configurations between different temperatures to escape energy barriers.
  • Introduce the Wang-Landau algorithm for directly calculating the density of states $g(E)$.

Chapter 8

Finance II: The Stochastic Calculus (SDEs)


  • Show why classical calculus fails for random walks, where the core rule is $(dW_t)^2 = dt$.
  • Introduce the Wiener Process $W_t$ and the Stochastic Differential Equation (SDE).
  • Derive Itō's Lemma, the stochastic chain rule, which introduces a new $\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial S^2}$ term.
  • Implement the Euler-Maruyama method as the base numerical solver for SDEs.

Finance III: Black-Scholes-Merton (BSM)


  • Derive the BSM deterministic PDE by constructing a risk-free "delta-hedged" portfolio.
  • Show how all stochastic $dW_t$ terms perfectly cancel, removing all randomness from the portfolio's value.
  • Recognize the BSM equation as mathematically equivalent to the Heat/Diffusion Equation (from Vol I).

Biology II: Neuroscience (Hodgkin-Huxley)
